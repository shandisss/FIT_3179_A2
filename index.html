MALAYSIA’S MEAT CONSUMPTION LANDSCAPE

An exploration of meat demand, self-sufficiency, and trade – from global context to local capacity.

Poultry dominates globally

High consumption in Southeast Asia and North America.

Farming the Demand

Malaysia’s farm registry is dominated by ruminants – especially cattle and goats – with Kelantan and Terengganu leading beef farm counts.

Pig farms are few and concentrated in Pulau Pinang, exposing supply to regional risk.

The registry omits industrial poultry operations, implying large-scale production is managed separately.

This highlights Malaysia’s dual system: commercial poultry vs smallholder ruminants.

Consumption Trends by Meat Type

Between 2014–2020, poultry stayed the top protein, though with a mild decline. Pork remained stable, demographically tied to non-Muslim consumers. Beef and mutton declined modestly, showing the effects of limited supply and cultural preference. The market overall appears saturated, with demand peaking post-2016.

Yearly Meat Share Snapshot

Malaysia’s meat consumption is overwhelmingly poultry-based, consistently accounting for 70–80% of total intake. This dominance reflects poultry’s affordability, availability, and cultural acceptability across communities. Pork, beef, and mutton contribute much smaller shares, with pork intake concentrated among the non-Muslim population. Year-on-year shifts are minor, suggesting that consumer preferences remain steady despite supply fluctuations.

Can We Feed Ourselves?

Malaysia produces enough poultry and nearly enough pork to meet demand. But beef and mutton lag far behind, with SSRs below 30%. This structural reliance on imports underscores vulnerability in ruminant supply and signals where investment is lacking.

The SSR hierarchy also aligns closely with per-capita consumption levels, reflecting how supply capacity reinforces dietary preferences.

Imports Bridge the Gap

Value-Based Trade Insights
Malaysia’s livestock trade reveals a sharp imbalance: imports vastly outweigh exports in both value and diversity. In 2019, beef imports alone approached RM 2.4 billion, highlighting Malaysia’s heavy reliance on international ruminant supply. Poultry, despite being self-sufficient domestically, still shows rising import values — particularly in processed forms which suggests growing demand for value-added or specialty poultry products. Exports remain limited, focused on live ducks and live poultry to niche ASEAN markets, with minimal gains in high-value categories.

Quantity-Based Trade Patterns
When measured by volume, the story remains consistent: imports fill the ruminant supply gap, with beef and mutton dominating inbound tonnes. Poultry volumes are also substantial, though this may reflect cross-border trade in live birds rather than supply shortfalls.

Exports, by contrast, are narrow and commodity-specific, dominated by live poultry and ducks. They show little year-on-year expansion. The overall pattern reinforces Malaysia’s dependence on meat imports, especially for beef and mutton, and highlights untapped export potential.
